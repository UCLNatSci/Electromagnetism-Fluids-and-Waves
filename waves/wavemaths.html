1.1. Mathematical Preliminaries¶

Since waves involve changes in both space and time \(u(x,\,t)\), the definitions of first and second partial derivatives in terms of limits are useful here:

(1.1)¶\[\begin{split}\frac{\partial u(x,t)}{\partial x} &=& \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{u(x + \Delta x,t) - u(x,t)}{\Delta x} \\ \frac{\partial^2 u(x,t)}{\partial x^2} &=& \frac{\partial}{\partial x}\left(\frac{\partial u(x,t)}{\partial x}\right) \\ &=& \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\Delta x}\left[\frac{\partial u(x+\Delta x,t)}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial u(x,t)}{\partial x} \right] \\ &=& \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{\Delta x}\left[\frac{u(x + 2\Delta x,t) - u(x+ \Delta x,t)}{\Delta x} - \frac{u(x + \Delta x,t) - u(x,t)}{\Delta x} \right] \\\end{split}\]

Which means we can write a second partial derivative using a limit definition as:

(1.2)¶\[\frac{\partial^2 u(x,t)}{\partial x^2} = \lim_{\Delta x \rightarrow 0} \frac{u(x+2\Delta x,t) - 2u(x+\Delta x,t) + u(x,t)}{{\Delta x}^2} \]
